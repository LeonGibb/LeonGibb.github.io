WELCOME LETTER

Dear friends, fellow Rhinologists and members of ERS,

I am delighted to join Professor Par Stjärne in welcoming you to beautiful Stockholm, the ‘Venice’ of the north. We know that an impressive number of participants have registered from all over the world and all sharing a common interest in rhinology and its many allied subjects.

As in the past, this 26th European Rhinologic Society meeting will combine forces with IRS and ISIAN to provide a wide ranging exposition of all things sinonasal and beyond, to the interfaces with the skull base, orbit and lower respiratory tract, to encompass immunology and allergy, sleep apnoea and septorhinoplasty - in fact to provide ‘state of the art’ updates on basic science, translational and clinical advances which will be of help to rhinologists at all stages of their career. The title ‘Rhinological Disorders – a health priority for the future’ is particularly appropriate given the scientific and economic challenges we presently face throughout the world. Professor Stjärne, his local organising committee, Professor Fokkens and the ERS Scientific Committee have worked hard over the last two years to make this congress as memorable, innovative and enjoyable as possible and I am looking forward to a meeting of great intellectual and practical value, in a city of great culture, history and beauty and above all, the opportunity to rekindle friendships for which rhinology is justifiably famous.

Valerie J Lund CBE Prof. Pär Stjärne,

Secretary General, ERS President of the
ERS/ISIAN/IRS 2016 Congress
